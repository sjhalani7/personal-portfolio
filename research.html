MY RESEARCH EXPERIENCE


During my time at Santa Clara University, I was able to get involved with some research projects while pursuing my degree.

This page highlights my contributions to the research project and our motivation for the research.

Research Lead

Online Hate Speech Spreader Detection using Modified PageRank Algorithm

October 2021 - Present

  • Lead team of three people to detect & flag hate speech spreaders on online social networks using Python & libraries like sklearn
  • Modify Google’s PageRank algorithm to implement a graph-based model using adjacency matrices to calculate user influence & measure hate speech spread within a user network using data & network analysis on pre-trained machine learning models
  • Co-author research paper detailing significant findings, accepted for presentation at the 2023 International DSML conference

Getting involved with research was on my bucket list for my first year, but I never thought I’d be able to get involved with it as much as I did! During a meeting for all computer science majors, I was fortunate to have an amazing conversation with Dr. Smita Ghosh, a professor of computer science specializing in data science.

As we talked about her research on detecting fake news online, I reflected on some horrendously racist comments faced by a group of English soccer players following England’s loss to Italy in the European championship of 2022, and proposed an idea to start a research project on detecting hate speech on as social media platforms.

The project has evolved a lot since we started, and we recently submitted a paper outlining our findings to the 4th International Conference on Data Science and Machine Learning, which got accepted for publication! Check out the conference and a snippet of the to-be published article below!

Research Assistant

TailorEd: Augmenting Pedagogy using Machine Learning

November 2021 - September 2023

  • Examined relationship between pedagogical techniques and student performance using machine learning
  • Developed APIs using Flask for an effective link between the front-end website tool & back-end database of analytical results

A project already in full flow when I got involved, TailorEd caught my eye because of how interesting the idea was: analyzing class images and student performances to see which methods of teaching are most effective.

Under the mentorship of my lab professor, I took charge of the database management for TailorEd, diving into complex SQL tasks and developing data pipelines. This research assistant role was my entry into the world of data handling!

My role eventually evolved over time, with my focus shifting to developing APIs for efficient data transfer, facilitating the dynamic visualization of our educational insights on the front end.
