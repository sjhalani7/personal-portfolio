MY CAMPUS INVOLVEMENTS


Beyond my academics, I make an effort to get involved with numerous clubs and societies on campus.

This page talks about my roles in the various different organizations I have had the opportunity to be a part of during my time at Santa Clara University.

Director of Public Relations

Student Advisory Board - Ciocca Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship

September 2023 - Present

  • As Director of Public Relations, I focus on keeping both undergraduate and graduate students informed and engaged through a bi-quarterly newsletter, updating them on upcoming events, application deadlines, and recruitment opportunities.
  • I am also responsible for documenting board meeting minutes and providing comprehensive overviews of all events, which allows for smooth planning of the events we host!

These responsibilities not only kept the student body connected but also honed my skills in communication and organization, ensuring a well-informed and engaged community.

Ciocca Center’s mission is to instill and expand the entrepreneurial mindset in SCU students, faculty, staff and alumni, throughout Silicon Valley and across the world. Ciocca Center embraces an inclusive approach that engages responsible innovation to address pressing human needs and create long-term value within our communities.

The Student Advisory Board, which is comprised of students from a broad range of disciplines at both the graduate and undergraduate level, provides a formal governance connection to students. The Student Advisory Board is chartered to ensure our programs are relevant and useful to students campus-wide, and to identify and suggest opportunities for interdisciplinary interaction.

Director of Communications

Association of Computing Machinery

April 2023 - Present

  • In my role as the Director of Communications for ACM, my primary responsibility involves overseeing the industry liaisons for both ACM and its women's chapter, ACM-W. My focus is on ensuring they effectively secure sponsors for the clubs, particularly for Hack4Humanity, Santa Clara University's largest hackathon.
  • In addition to this, I manage all club communications, which includes liaising with other clubs and organizations, diligently checking and responding to emails, and maintaining a smooth and efficient flow of information in and out of the club.
  • Last year, I worked as the industry liaison for ACM, developing impactful relationships by leading communications with over 10+companies, securing over $18,000 in sponsorships for the biggest hackathon we had ever hosted!

This role has not only honed my leadership and coordination skills but also deepened my understanding of effective communication strategies within a dynamic, club-based environment.

Our goal at ACM is for our members is to develop real-world CS skills and experience, to promote professional development though jobs and internships, and to cultivate a tight-knit collaborative community

At SCU's ACM, we present informative workshops, bring guest speakers, and host fun weekly meetings, as well as hackathons. We strive to make a community where people can find friends with similar interests and grow their real world technical knowledge to be more effective in industry.

Professional Development Chair

Theta Tau Professional Engineering Fraternity

April 2023 - Present

  • In my role as the Professional Development Chair of Theta Tau, my core responsibility is to ensure that our active members receive the necessary professional development to excel in their engineering careers. This involves hosting 2-3 official professional development events each quarter, each tailored to encompass a variety of engineering disciplines.
  • In addition, I manage a living document detailing ongoing professional development opportunities, ensuring members have continuous access to valuable resources, alongside fostering strong relationships with the SCU Career Center, creating a bridge between our members and broader career development resources.
  • I also offer personalized support to members through conducting resume reviews.

This role not only allows me to contribute to the professional growth of my peers but also enhances my skills in event planning, relationship building, and providing tailored career guidance within the engineering field.

Founded at the University of Minnesota in 1904, Theta Tau is the nation's oldest and foremost Fraternity for Engineers. Theta Tau carefully follows a program in the selection and development of its members that stresses the importance of high professional ethics and exemplary practices.

Within each chapter, the Fraternity stimulates professional activity and social compatibility; provides a framework for group participation in campus, community, engineering, and fraternity affairs; and promotes lasting friendships - a lifetime of brotherhood in an engineering environment.

Peer Career Advisor

Santa Clara University Career Center

September 2022 - Present

  • As a Peer Career Advisor at SCU's Career Center, I interact with external recruiters to facilitate on-campus interviewing, networking sessions, and career fairs, alongside coaching students on career strategies and methods to leverage existing tools and career center services on a daily basis.
  • I am also responsible for analyzing career fair attendee data, encompassing over 9500 students and more than 800 employers. This analysis is vital in understanding and improving the effectiveness of the career fairs.

My work at the career center not only aims to helps students in their professional journey but it has also enhanced my skills in data analysis, event coordination, and effective communication & teamwork

The Career Center model embraces Santa Clara’s tradition of developing ethical leaders of compassion and social consciousness and fosters participation across the Santa Clara community in order to support every student in their professional journey.

We empower students to explore and develop awareness through reflection and practical experience so they are prepared to make thoughtful decisions about career in alignment with who they are throughout their lifetime. We achieve this by providing robust and meaningful resources and by engaging the Santa Clara ecosystem, Silicon Valley and beyond.

University Honors Program

September 2021 - Present

  • As a member of the Honors Program at Santa Clara University, I am immersed in a unique academic experience that involves seminar-style, learner-directed classes. These classes are highly interactive, and I actively participate in them, enjoying the dynamic and engaging learning environment.
  • A significant component of this program is the opportunity to undertake a senior thesis. This project allows me to delve into in-depth research on a topic of my choice, under the close guidance and collaboration of one or more faculty mentors.

Being a part of the honors program is an incredible learning experience where I get to interact with like-minded individuals and grow not just as a student, but as a person as well!

The University Honors Program is a community of independent thinkers committed to the pursuit of academic excellence and global engagement.

It promotes intellectual excellence, critical thinking, and global engagement through seminar-style courses, difficult dialogues, and mentored independent research.
